# Global Banking Sector Stability Analysis Using IMF Financial Soundness Indicators

## Overview

This project analyzes banking sector stability across countries using the International Monetary Fund's Financial Soundness Indicators (FSIs).

The objective is to assess the resilience and health of banking systems by examining key indicators related to capital adequacy, asset quality, profitability, liquidity, and exposure to market risk.

---

## Project Objective

The project aims to evaluate banking sector strength across countries and regions by identifying trends, vulnerabilities, and areas of resilience within the financial system.

The analysis supports evidence-based assessments of financial sector stability and macro-financial risk.

---

## Data Source

### International Monetary Fund (IMF)

Financial Soundness Indicators (FSIs)

The dataset contains internationally comparable indicators used to monitor the health and stability of financial institutions.

---

## Key Indicators Analyzed

### Capital Adequacy

* Regulatory Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets
* Tier 1 Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets

### Asset Quality

* Nonperforming Loans to Total Gross Loans
* Nonperforming Loans Net of Provisions to Capital

### Profitability

* Return on Assets (ROA)
* Return on Equity (ROE)

### Liquidity

* Liquid Assets to Total Assets
* Liquid Assets to Short-Term Liabilities

### Market Risk Exposure

* Net Open Position in Foreign Exchange to Capital
